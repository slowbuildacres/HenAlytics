How many eggs, realistically

The widely published figure is 200–280 eggs per year, which lands around 4–5 eggs per hen per week. That's a solid layer — though not a record-setter. Lighter production breeds like Leghorns and hybrid sex-links will out-lay an Orpington. People keep Buffs for the whole package: calm temperament, cold-hardiness, dual-purpose size, and strong mothering instinct.

Two things to keep in mind about that yearly number:

  • Age matters. Production peaks in the first laying year and then drops roughly 10–15% each year as a hen ages.
  • The year isn't even. Backyard hens slow sharply over winter's short days and pause to molt in the fall, then ramp back up in spring. The yearly total is real, but you won't see an egg a day from every hen, every day.
